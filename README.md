## § Abstract

> My research addresses the principled design of **dependable, efficient machine learning systems under operational uncertainty and regulatory constraint.** Current work spans: *(1)* ML infrastructure architectures for financial AI achieving sub-second latency at terabyte scale with formal reliability guarantees; *(2)* bio-inspired vision transformers incorporating signal processing (EMD, temporal attention) for robust low-signal sensing; *(3)* governance-aware data pipelines integrating automated quality validation, lineage tracking, and compliance constraints.
>
> I bring convergent depth — both theoretical *(peer-reviewed publication in **Nature**, rigorous algorithms training)* and empirical *(five years designing production systems for fraud detection and risk modeling in regulated financial environments)*. I seek doctoral training to formalize systems-level thinking around ML reliability and develop generalizable frameworks bridging theoretical ML and operational reality.

## § 2. &nbsp; Research Statement

> *"The hardest problems in ML deployment are not modeling or statistical in nature — they are* ***systems problems:*** *feature representation stability under temporal drift, latency constraints from downstream regulatory systems, and provenance & auditability for financial compliance. These concerns are underexplored in the ML research literature but are increasingly central to ML Systems and Responsible AI as subdisciplines."*

**Systems perspective on ML reliability** — At Bank of America, I architected AI-driven pipelines processing multi-terabyte financial transaction streams for fraud detection. The insight: correctness, auditability, and efficiency guarantees don't emerge naturally as systems scale — they must be designed in from first principles.

**Bio-inspired signal processing** — NOCT-A-VIS applies signal processing principles inspired by biological systems (the owl's visual cortex) to improve robustness under low-signal conditions — bridging neuroscience, classical signal processing, and modern deep learning into a unified architecture.

**Doctoral objectives** — A PhD will formalize these practitioner-informed insights into rigorous, generalizable frameworks. Seeking faculty whose work bridges systems infrastructure and machine learning: latency-aware feature stores, automated data quality validation, certified robustness in deployed models.
